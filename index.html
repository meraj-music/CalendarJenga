Calendar Jenga

Pick the time window, add invitee email address(es), and let Calendar Jenga sort your meetings based on everyone's availability.

Connect to Google Calendar

Stored in your browser only. Never sent anywhere except Google.

Not connected
One-time free setup, ~5 min

Someone on your team does this once. After that, everyone just clicks "Continue with Google."

  1. Open console.cloud.google.com and create a new project (or pick an existing one).
  2. In the left sidebar, go to APIs & Services → Library. Search for Google Calendar API and click Enable.
  3. Go to Google Auth Platform → Branding (left sidebar). Set:
    — App name: Calendar Jenga
    — User support email: your work email
    — Audience: Internal (lets anyone in your Google Workspace org sign in, no approval needed). If you want people outside your org to use it too, choose External and click Publish App after saving.
    — Developer contact email: your work email
    — Hit Save.
  4. Go to Google Auth Platform → Data Access. Click Add or Remove Scopes, search freebusy, check the box for Google Calendar API — calendar.freebusy, click Update, then Save.
  5. Go to Google Auth Platform → Clients. Click Create Client.
    — Application type: Web application
    — Name: anything (e.g. "Calendar Jenga Web")
    — Under Authorized JavaScript origins, click Add URI and enter: https://meraj-music.github.io
    — Leave Redirect URIs empty. Click Create.
  6. Copy the Client ID from the confirmation screen. Paste it in the field above. Share it with your team so they can paste it too.

What does this tool access? Only free/busy times (scope: calendar.freebusy). It cannot read event titles, descriptions, attendees, or anything else. It cannot create, edit, or delete events. Everything runs in your browser — no data is sent to any server except Google.
